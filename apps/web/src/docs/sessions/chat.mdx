# Chat Interface Details

This page expands on the **Sessions & Chat** documentation and focuses on how to read and use the chat interface inside ArcAgentic.

---

## Layout Overview

When you open a session, you will typically see:

- **Conversation history** – Messages from you and the NPC(s)
- **Input box** – Where you type what you say or do
- **Send button** – Sends your message if you prefer clicking over pressing Enter
- **Character panel** – Basic info about the active NPC (name, role, short summary)
- **Setting panel** – A brief reminder of where the scene is happening

Some layouts may also show:

- A selector to choose which NPC you are talking to
- A debug or info panel for advanced users

---

## Writing Messages

You can treat the chat box like a mix of speech and narration.

- Plain text is treated as spoken dialogue:
  - `"Hello, how was your day?"`
- You can describe actions or thoughts using `*asterisks*` (for example, `*I sit down at the bar*`). The system uses these to better understand what is happening.

The AI considers:

- What you say
- What you do
- What the character already knows about you and the setting

---

## Working with Multiple NPCs

In some sessions, you may have more than one NPC present.

- Look for an **NPC selector** near the input or at the top of the chat
- You can often choose:
  - **Auto** – Let the system decide who replies
  - A specific character – Force all replies to come from that NPC

This is useful when:

- You want to talk only to one character in a crowded scene
- You are testing how different personalities respond to the same situation

---

## Reading Responses

NPC messages are shaped by:

- Their **personality traits** and **memories**
- The **setting** description and current location
- The **recent conversation** and your last message

If a response feels off:

- Check that the character's traits and description match what you expect
- Make sure the setting description matches the scene you imagine
- Try rephrasing your message with a bit more detail

---

## Using the Debug Panel (Optional)

If you enable the debug or developer view, you may see extra information such as:

- How your input was classified (speech, action, emotion, sensory, etc.)
- Which internal agents were involved
- How long different steps took

You do **not** need this to play, but it can help you understand:

- Why a character reacted a certain way
- Whether your configuration is working as expected

---

## Tips for Smoother Play

- **Stay in character:** Write messages as if you are your player character
- **Reference the environment:** Mention objects, sounds, or people from the setting
- **Build continuity:** Refer back to earlier events so the conversation feels like a single story
- **Adjust pacing:** You can zoom in on small moments or skip ahead with a single sentence
