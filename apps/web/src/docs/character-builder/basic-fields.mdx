# Basic Fields

This page explains the core fields in the Character Builder's **Basics** section. These are the foundational details that define who your character is.

---

## ID

The ID is a unique identifier for your character, used internally by the system.

- Must be unique across all your characters
- In production, this will be a system-generated UUID

> Tip: Use a combination of the character's name and role for easy identification (e.g., `innkeeper-sarah`, `wizard-eldrin`).

---

## Name

The character's display name as it appears in conversations and the UI.

- Can include spaces, capitals, and special characters
- Should be easy to remember and pronounce
- Can be a full name ("Elena Thornwood"), simple name ("Marcus"), or title ("The Wanderer")
- Used in AI prompts to refer to the character

**Examples:**

- "Elena Thornwood"
- "Captain Marcus"
- "The Old Wizard"
- "Sara"

---

## Age (Optional)

The character's age in years. This field is optional but helps shape the character's voice and behavior.

- Young characters may sound more energetic or inexperienced
- Older characters may be wiser, more patient, or world-weary
- Used in AI prompts to inform dialogue style and knowledge

**Tips:**

- You can use a specific number (27) or a general age range (mid-40s) in your mental model
- Consider how age affects the character's perspective and life experience

---

## Gender (Optional)

The character's gender identity. Used for pronouns and descriptions.

- Optional field - leave blank for neutral presentation
- Freeform text field - use any label that fits ("female", "male", "non-binary", etc.)
- Included in AI prompts when provided
- Affects how the character is described by the system

---

## Tags

Comma-separated keywords for organizing and filtering your character library.

**Important:** Tags are for **UI organization only**. They help you search and filter characters but are NOT used in gameplay or AI prompts.

**Good tag examples:**

- Role tags: `merchant`, `guard`, `noble`, `healer`
- Location tags: `tavern`, `marketplace`, `castle`
- Genre tags: `fantasy`, `urban`, `horror`
- Attribute tags: `friendly`, `mysterious`, `humorous`

**Best practices:**

- Use lowercase for consistency
- Keep tags short and searchable
- Use 3-7 tags per character
- Be consistent across your library

Default: `draft` (automatically added to new characters)

---

## Summary

A brief description of the character for browsing your character library.

**Important:** This field is for **UI display only** - it helps you identify characters in your library but is NOT used in AI prompts or gameplay.

- Keep it concise (1-3 sentences)
- Focus on what makes this character distinctive
- Think of it as a "character card" summary

**Example:**

> A weathered blacksmith known for honest work and fair prices. Gruff exterior but kind to apprentices.

---

## Backstory

The character's history and background. This field **is used in AI prompts** and directly affects how the character behaves.

- Aim for 2-5 sentences covering key life events
- Include formative experiences that shaped who they are
- Mention relationships, past conflicts, or defining moments
- Keep it focused on what's relevant to the character's current behavior

**Example:**

> Elena grew up in a mining town, learning blacksmithing from her father. After his death in a cave-in, she took over the forge at age 19. Years of hard work and dealing with difficult customers have made her suspicious of nobility, though she has a soft spot for struggling apprentices.

**Note:** The backstory field will be refactored in the future to support richer, more structured background information. For now, treat it as a free-text narrative.
