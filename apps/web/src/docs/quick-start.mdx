# Quick Start Guide

Get up and running with ArcAgentic in just a few minutes!

## Step 1: Create Your First Character

1. Click **Characters** in the sidebar (or press `C`)
2. Click **Create New Character**
3. Fill in the basic information:
   - **ID** - Unique identifier (e.g., `aria-wanderer`)
   - **Name** - Character's display name
   - **Summary** - Brief description for your library
   - **Backstory** - Key history that shapes behavior
   - **Personality** - Quick traits (e.g., "curious, friendly, cautious")

### Example Character

```
ID: aria-wanderer
Name: Aria the Wanderer
Summary: A seasoned traveler with stories from across the realm
Backstory: Aria has traveled the realm for ten years, collecting stories and
  learning languages. She's seen both wonders and horrors, making her curious
  about people but cautious with strangers.
Personality: curious, friendly, cautious with strangers, loves storytelling
```

4. Optional: Adjust **Big Five** sliders for more nuanced personality
5. Click **Save** to create your character

## Step 2: Create a Setting

1. Click **Settings** in the sidebar (or press `S`)
2. Click **Create New Setting**
3. Define your world:
   - **Name** - The name of your setting
   - **Description** - What makes this place unique?
   - **Atmosphere** - The mood and feel

### Example Setting

```
Name: The Moonlit Tavern
Description: A cozy inn at a crossroads where travelers share tales
Atmosphere: Warm, welcoming, mysterious
```

4. Click **Save** to create your setting

## Step 3: Start a Session

1. Click **Sessions** in the sidebar
2. Click **New Session**
3. Select your character and setting
4. Click **Start Session**

## Step 4: Begin Chatting

Once in the session:

1. Type your message in the chat input
2. Press **Enter** or click **Send**
3. Watch as your character responds based on their personality and the setting

### Tips for Better Interactions

- **Be specific** - Detailed messages get more detailed responses
- **Ask questions** - Characters love to share their knowledge
- **Reference the setting** - Mention the environment to get contextual responses
- **Build on previous exchanges** - Characters remember your conversation
